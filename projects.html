Android Calibration App

During my research internship at UC Berkeley this past summer, I wrote an Android app that displays precise dynamic patterns on a phone screen and is used for calibrating light field photography equipment. Before this project, I did not have any Android experience so I taught myself by following online tutorials and reading Stackoverflow if I had other questions. I also learned how to incorporate user feedback as I built my app by showing my mentor the app at each stage and taking into account his comments. When I had my first working version done in two weeks, I watched my mentor use it in the lab so that I could figure out what other features I could add to the app to make it even more useful and easier to use.
